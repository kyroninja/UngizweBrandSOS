About Us

Mzansi Cries for Help is an anonymous platform where customers, employees, and community members can share their experiences with South African brands. Many concerns go unheard until they become crises. Customers struggle with poor service, employees face unresolved workplace issues, and communities are often left without a voice. We believe these experiences are valuable signals that deserve attention. Our platform allows people to safely and anonymously submit a "cry for help" about a brand. Each cry contributes to a collective score that helps identify patterns, highlight recurring concerns, and reveal the real experiences behind public perception. Our goal is not to shame businesses, but to encourage transparency, accountability, and positive change. By giving people a voice and surfacing trends that might otherwise remain hidden, we hope to help build stronger relationships between brands, employees, and the communities they serve.

Understanding How it Works

At Mzansi Cries for Help, every submission becomes part of a larger pattern. We collect anonymous experiences and transform them into meaningful signals that help reveal how brands are truly performing across customers, employees, and communities.

01What is considered "a cry for help"?

A cry for help is any honest experience involving a South African brand—whether you are a customer, employee, or service provider. It can include poor service, unfair treatment, workplace issues, or repeated frustrations that deserve attention. Every submission is anonymous and contributes to a larger picture of brand behaviour over time.

02 How is my information protected?

We do not request or store personally identifiable information. Reports are designed to be anonymous by default. Your safety and privacy are central to the platform, especially for sensitive employee-related submissions. We focus on patterns, not individuals.

03What happens to my submission after I send it?

Your report is added to an aggregated “brand signal score,” which helps identify recurring issues and emerging trends. Individual reports are not judged in isolation, but instead contribute to a broader understanding of brand performance and accountability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to understand how anonymous “cries for help” become structured brand intelligence across customers, employees, and partners.

What is a “cry for help”?

A “cry for help” is an anonymous submission describing an experience with a brand — whether from a customer, employee, or partner. It can include service issues, workplace concerns, or repeated frustrations that deserve attention.

Is my submission really anonymous?

Yes. We do not collect personally identifiable information. Reports are designed to protect your identity while still contributing to aggregated brand insights and trend analysis.

Who can submit feedback?

Anyone with direct or indirect experience of a South African brand can submit a report — including customers, employees, contractors, and service providers.

What happens to my submission after I send it?

Your submission becomes part of a larger brand signal system. Individual reports are not judged in isolation — instead, they contribute to topic-level scores that highlight patterns over time.

Do brands see who submitted the report?

No. Reports are anonymised. Brands only see aggregated insights and recurring themes, not individual identities or submissions.
